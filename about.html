Objectives

The objectives of the Nepal Food Security Dashboard are to enhance transparency regarding food markets in Nepal by offering data and insights on key commodities, to support data-driven decision-making for policymakers and other stakeholders, and to facilitate more in-depth analysis. In addition, the Dashboard aims to monitor trends in food prices to inform planning, identify unusual price shocks and their likely causes, foster collaboration among stakeholders, and disseminate relevant information through monthly bulletins.

Acknowlegement

This work is supported by the USAID/Nepal-funded Cereal Systems Initiative for South Asia (CSISA) ‘Building food system resilience to global supply chain and climate shocks in Nepal’ Activity and CSISA Phase III supported by USAID-Washington. CSISA’s is also aligned with the One CGIAR Regional Integrated Initiative Transforming Agrifood Systems in South Asia (TAFSSA). CSISA is led by the International Center for Improvement of Maize and Wheat (CIMMYT) and implemented jointly with the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI), the Institute for Integrated Development Studies (IIDS), the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI), and the International Water Management Institute (IWMI). International Development Enterprises (iDE) is another key partner of CSISA in Nepal and Bangladesh. The dashboard was designed and developed by Soonho Kim and Nicholas Minot of IFPRI with input from Timothy Krupnik, CSISA project director, other CSISA project staff, and USAID/Nepal.
